# Federal Court Rules and Procedure Reference

Key rules, deadlines, and standards for federal civil, criminal, appellate, and sentencing proceedings.

**Knowledge base:** Through August 2025 | Always verify amendments at uscourts.gov before relying on any provision.

## Federal Rules of Civil Procedure — Key Provisions

### Rule 3 — Commencing a Civil Action
Filing the complaint tolls the SOL for federal claims. For state law claims in federal court, check state law (Erie doctrine) — some states require service, not just filing.

### Rule 4 — Service of Process
- **90-day limit** for service (Rule 4(m)); must dismiss or extend if missed
- Service methods: personal delivery, leaving at dwelling, authorized agent, state law methods
- **Waiver of service:** Defendant has 30 days to return waiver (gets 60-day answer period instead of 21)

### Rule 8 — Pleading Standards (Twombly/Iqbal)
- Short and plain statement showing entitlement to relief
- *Twombly* (2007): Plead facts sufficient to state a plausible claim
- *Iqbal* (2009): (1) Disregard conclusory allegations; (2) Remaining facts must plausibly suggest liability
- Affirmative defenses must be pled or they are waived

### Rule 11 — Sanctions
Attorney certifies: not for improper purpose; legal contentions warranted; factual contentions have evidentiary support.
- **Safe harbor:** Serve motion on opposing party 21 days before filing — they can cure/withdraw
- No safe harbor for court-initiated sanctions
- Sanctions aimed at deterrence, not compensation

### Rule 12 — Defenses and Motions
- **Answer deadline:** 21 days after service (60 days after waiver of service)
- 12(b) defenses: (1) subject matter jurisdiction (NEVER waived), (2-5) personal jurisdiction/venue/process (waived if not raised first), (6) failure to state claim, (7) failure to join party
- All 12(b) defenses must be consolidated in first pre-answer motion

### Rule 15 — Amended Pleadings
- **Amendment as of right:** Within 21 days after serving pleading or after responsive pleading/12 motion
- After: need court leave — "freely given when justice so requires" (*Foman factors*: undue delay, bad faith, prejudice, futility)
- **Relation back (15(c)):** Amendment relates back if same conduct/transaction/occurrence; for party changes, new party must have received notice within the Rule 4(m) period (90 days)

### Rule 23 — Class Actions
**Prerequisites (all four):** Numerosity (40+ members typically), Commonality, Typicality, Adequacy of representation

**Types:** (b)(1) inconsistent adjudications risk; (b)(2) injunctive/declaratory (no opt-out); (b)(3) common questions predominate + superior method (opt-out required)

Settlement must be "fair, reasonable, and adequate"; court must approve.

### Rule 26 — Discovery Scope
**Scope:** Any nonprivileged matter relevant to claims/defenses and **proportional to the needs of the case**

**Proportionality factors:** Importance of issues, amount in controversy, relative access to information, parties' resources, importance of discovery, burden vs. benefit

**Work product doctrine:** Documents prepared in anticipation of litigation:
- Ordinary: discoverable with substantial need + undue hardship
- Opinion (mental impressions, legal theories): near-absolute protection

**Meet and confer:** Must occur 21 days before scheduling conference; parties discuss ESI, preservation, privilege issues, discovery plan; report due 14 days after conference.

**Supplementation:** Must supplement responses when party learns they are incomplete/incorrect.

### Rules 30-34 — Discovery Mechanics
- **Depositions:** Max 10 per side; max 7 hours per witness; 1 deposition per witness
- **Rule 30(b)(6):** Organization must designate witness to testify on specified matters; testimony binds organization
- **Interrogatories:** Max 25 per party (including subparts); 30-day response deadline
- **Document requests:** 30-day response deadline; objections must be specific (general objections improper)

### Rule 37 — Discovery Sanctions
- **Motion to compel:** Must certify good faith conference first; prevailing party generally gets fees
- **Sanctions for order violation:** May include facts taken as established, prohibit use, strike pleadings, dismiss, default
- **Spoliation of ESI (Rule 37(e)):**
  - Prejudice only → curative measures
  - **Intent to deprive** → presumption of adverse inference, or dismissal/default (severe)
  - Mere negligence does NOT justify Rule 37(e)(2) sanctions

### Rule 56 — Summary Judgment
Standard: No genuine dispute of material fact + movant entitled to judgment as a matter of law (*Celotex*, 1986)

Court views all facts and inferences in light most favorable to non-movant.

**Burden shifting:** Movant shows absence of genuine dispute; if non-movant bears burden of proof, non-movant must produce specific facts showing genuine dispute.

### Rule 65 — Injunctions
**Four-factor test for preliminary injunction (*Winter*, 2008):**
1. Likelihood of success on merits
2. Likely irreparable harm without relief
3. Balance of equities tips in movant's favor
4. Public interest

**TRO without notice:** Specific facts showing immediate irreparable injury; certification of notice attempts; expires after 14 days

## Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure — Key Provisions

### Rule 6 — Grand Jury
- 16-23 members; 12 must concur to indict; quorum of 16 to hear evidence
- Grand jury proceedings are secret (Rule 6(e)) — contempt for disclosure
- Standard for indictment: probable cause (much lower than trial)
- No defense presence; hearsay admissible

### Rule 7 — Indictment
- Required for felonies (5th Amendment); waivable for felonies with defendant's written consent
- Must state essential facts constituting the offense; must cite statute

### Rule 11 — Pleas
Before accepting guilty plea, court must address defendant personally on all rights being waived, maximum penalties, immigration consequences, and non-binding nature of sentencing recommendations.

**Plea agreements:**
- (c)(1)(A): Charge dismissal
- (c)(1)(B): Sentencing recommendation (not binding on court)
- (c)(1)(C): Specific sentence binding on court if accepted

**Withdrawal:** Before acceptance: any reason; after acceptance: fair and just reason; after sentencing: manifest injustice only

### Rule 16 — Criminal Discovery
Government must disclose: defendant's statements, prior criminal record, documents material to defense, examination/test results.

**Brady/Giglio (constitutional obligations):** Government must disclose evidence materially favorable to defendant, including impeachment material for government witnesses.

**Jencks Act (18 U.S.C. § 3500):** Prior statements of government witnesses must be produced after the witness testifies on direct.

### Rule 29 — Motion for Acquittal
Standard: Could any rational trier of fact find essential elements of crime beyond reasonable doubt (*Jackson v. Virginia*, 1979)?

Must make Rule 29(a) motion before case submitted to jury to preserve Rule 29(c) post-verdict motion right.

### Rule 32 — Sentencing
- Defendant has **right of allocution** (to speak at sentencing)
- Court must consider 18 U.S.C. § 3553(a) factors
- PSR disclosed 35 days before sentencing; defendant has 14 days to object

## Federal Rules of Evidence — Key Standards

### Relevance and Exclusion (Rules 401-403)
- **401:** Any tendency to make a fact of consequence more or less probable — very low threshold
- **403:** Court may exclude if probative value **substantially outweighed** by danger of unfair prejudice, confusion, misleading jury, undue delay, or cumulative evidence

### Character Evidence (Rule 404)
- **General rule:** Character evidence inadmissible to prove action in conformity (propensity prohibition)
- **Prior bad acts (404(b)):** Admissible for non-propensity purposes: Motive, Opportunity, Intent, Preparation, Plan, Knowledge, Identity, Absence of mistake (mnemonic: **MOIPPKIA**)
- **Notice:** Prosecution must give reasonable pre-trial notice of 404(b) evidence

### Expert Testimony (Rule 702 — Daubert Standard)
Trial court is "gatekeeper"; expert testimony must be:
1. Based on sufficient facts or data
2. Product of reliable principles and methods
3. Expert reliably applied methods to case facts

**Daubert reliability factors:** Testing, peer review, error rate, general acceptance in scientific community

*Kumho Tire* (1999): Daubert applies to ALL expert testimony, not just scientific.

### Hearsay (Rules 801-807)

**Definition:** Out-of-court statement offered to prove the truth of the matter asserted

**Non-hearsay:**
- Not offered for truth (notice, verbal acts, legally operative words)
- Prior inconsistent statement (under oath at prior proceeding) — admissible for truth (Rule 801(d)(1))
- Admission of party-opponent — admissible for truth (Rule 801(d)(2))

**Key Rule 803 exceptions (regardless of availability):**
- Present sense impression: statement made during or immediately after event
- Excited utterance: statement while under stress of startling event
- State of mind: then-existing mental/emotional/physical condition
- Medical diagnosis/treatment: statements for medical purposes (includes history and causation)
- Business records: made at or near time of transaction; by person with knowledge; in regular course of business
- Public records: activities of office, matters observed under legal duty (law enforcement observations inadmissible against criminal defendant)

**Rule 804 exceptions (require unavailability):**
- Former testimony (prior opportunity to cross-examine)
- Dying declaration (belief of imminent death; homicide/civil)
- Statement against interest (contrary to pecuniary/penal interest)

**Confrontation Clause (*Crawford*, 2004):** Testimonial hearsay inadmissible against criminal defendant unless declarant unavailable AND prior opportunity to cross-examine.

### Authentication (Rules 901-902)
- Standard: Evidence sufficient to support a finding the item is what claimed
- Rule 902: Self-authenticating documents (public records with seal, certified records, commercial paper, certified business records under Rule 902(11) — eliminates need for live custodian)

### Privileges (Rule 501)
Governed by federal common law. Recognized: attorney-client, work product, marital (two types), psychotherapist-patient (*Jaffee*, 1996), deliberative process, state secrets.

**No federal privilege:** physician-patient (except psychotherapist), accountant-client, parent-child

## Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure — Key Provisions

### Rule 4 — Notice of Appeal Deadlines
- **Civil:** 30 days from judgment entry (60 days if U.S. is a party)
- **Tolling:** Timely Rule 50(b), 52(b), 59, or 60 motions toll the appeal deadline
- **Criminal (defendant):** 14 days; **government:** 30 days
- **Premature notice:** Treated as filed day after judgment enters

### Rule 28 — Brief Requirements
Must contain: corporate disclosure, table of contents, table of authorities, jurisdictional statement, issues presented, statement of the case, summary of argument, argument with headings, conclusion, certificate of compliance.

### Rule 32 — Brief Format and Length
- **Principal briefs:** 13,000 words (or 30 pages)
- **Reply briefs:** 6,500 words (or 15 pages)
- Minimum 14-point font, 1-inch margins, double-spaced

### Rule 35 — En Banc
Favored only when necessary for circuit uniformity or question of exceptional importance. Petition due within 45 days (14 days criminal).

## US Sentencing Guidelines — Step-by-Step Calculation

**Post-*Booker* (2005):** Guidelines are advisory. Courts MUST calculate the correct range, then consider 18 U.S.C. § 3553(a) factors.

### Calculation Steps

**Step 1: Base Offense Level (BOL)** — From applicable chapter 2 guideline
- Fraud (§2B1.1): BOL 6 or 7
- Drug trafficking (§2D1.1): Based on drug type/quantity
- Armed robbery (§2B3.1): BOL 20
- Murder (§2A1.1): BOL 43

**Step 2: Specific Offense Characteristics** — Adjustments within the offense guideline (loss amount, drug quantity, weapon use, victim vulnerability)

**Step 3: Chapter 3 Adjustments**

| Adjustment | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Hate crime motivation | +3 |
| Vulnerable victim | +2 |
| Official victim | +6 |
| Organizer/leader of 5+ | +4 |
| Manager/supervisor | +3 |
| Minor participant | -2 |
| Minimal participant | -4 |
| Obstruction/perjury | +2 |
| Acceptance of responsibility (timely plea) | -2 or -3 |

**Step 4: Criminal History Category (CHC)**

Points system:
- 3 points: prior sentence >13 months imprisonment
- 2 points: prior sentence ≥60 days
- 1 point: other prior sentences (max 4)
- +2: committed offense while under criminal justice sentence

| CHC | Points |
|-----|--------|
| I | 0-1 |
| II | 2-3 |
| III | 4-6 |
| IV | 7-9 |
| V | 10-12 |
| VI | 13+ |

**Career Offender (§4B1.1):** Automatic CHC VI if: defendant 18+, instant offense is crime of violence or drug offense, two prior felony convictions of same type.

**Zero-Point Offenders (§4C1.1, effective Nov. 2023):** Defendants with 0 criminal history points meeting additional criteria receive 2-level reduction.

**Step 5: Find Guideline Range** — Intersection of offense level and CHC in sentencing table

**Step 6: Check mandatory minimums** — Statutory mandatory minimums override if higher

**Step 7: Departures** — Within guidelines framework
- Government's §5K1.1 motion (substantial assistance): court may depart any amount downward
- Upward: extreme conduct, inadequately captured characteristics
- Downward: aberrant behavior, diminished capacity, coercion

**Step 8: §3553(a) Variance** — Post-*Booker*, court may impose above/below-guidelines sentence based on:
1. Nature/circumstances of offense and history/characteristics of defendant
2. Seriousness, respect for law, just punishment
3. Adequate deterrence
4. Protect public
5. Rehabilitation needs
6. Guideline range (one of many factors)
7. Avoid unwarranted sentencing disparities
8. Provide restitution

**Appellate review:** Abuse of discretion (*Gall*, 2007); no presumption of unreasonableness for outside-guidelines sentence; district court must justify significant variance with sufficiently compelling reasons.

## Practical Notes for Legal AI Agents

- **Local rules** frequently modify FRCP/FRAP deadlines — always check the specific district's local rules
- **Individual judge standing orders** may impose additional requirements
- **Circuit splits** exist on many procedural and evidentiary questions — identify the controlling circuit before advising
- **Computation of time (FRCP 6):** Excludes triggering day; extends to next business day if deadline falls on weekend/holiday
- **ESI preservation** duties arise at "reasonably anticipated litigation" — before suit is filed
- **Litigation holds** must be implemented promptly; spoliation sanctions can be severe
- Pre-filing investigation is a professional responsibility obligation under Rule 11
